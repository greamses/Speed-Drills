Understanding Decimal Movement

When we multiply a decimal by 10, the decimal point moves one place to the right.

When we multiply by 100, the decimal point moves two places to the right.

5 Tenths

When we divide by 10, the decimal point moves one place to the left.

3 Ones
.
5 Tenths

3.5 ÷ 10 = 0.35

Remember the pattern:

  • × 10: 1 place right
  • × 100: 2 places right
  • × 1000: 3 places right
  • ÷ 10: 1 place left
  • ÷ 100: 2 places left
  • ÷ 1000: 3 places left
